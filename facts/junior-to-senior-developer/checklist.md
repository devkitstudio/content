## Am I Ready for Senior?

Rate yourself 1-5 on each (5 = strong):

### Technical Skills (Target: avg 4+)

- [ ] Can I architect new systems (not just code features)?
- [ ] Do I understand database design and trade-offs?
- [ ] Can I optimize performance (profiling, caching, queries)?
- [ ] Am I comfortable with distributed systems concepts?
- [ ] Can I design for scale (handle 10x growth)?
- [ ] Do I understand security implications of my code?
- [ ] Can I debug production issues effectively?
- [ ] Am I keeping up with important tech trends?

### System Thinking (Target: avg 4+)

- [ ] Do I understand how my code affects other teams?
- [ ] Can I see ripple effects of my decisions?
- [ ] Do I think about migrations and data consistency?
- [ ] Can I design for multiple failure modes?
- [ ] Do I understand cost implications of my code?
- [ ] Can I prioritize work by business impact?

### Soft Skills (Target: avg 4+)

- [ ] Can I communicate complex ideas to non-technical people?
- [ ] Have I mentored junior developers?
- [ ] Can I present ideas to leadership?
- [ ] Do I handle disagreement professionally?
- [ ] Can I unblock teammates without being asked?
- [ ] Am I trusted with critical decisions?
- [ ] Do I take initiative on hard problems?
- [ ] Have I owned outcomes (not just tasks)?

### Ownership (Target: avg 4+)

- [ ] Do I take responsibility for my code in production?
- [ ] Have I led a major project to completion?
- [ ] Do I anticipate problems before they happen?
- [ ] Am I proactive (not just reactive)?
- [ ] Can I manage a project across teams?
- [ ] Do I follow up on decisions made?
- [ ] Have I improved something in our system/process?

## If You Scored < 3 Avg

Focus on these areas:

**Low Technical Skills:**
- Pick a complex system
- Understand it deeply (architecture, data flow, performance)
- Make measurable improvements

**Low System Thinking:**
- Join architecture discussions
- Ask questions about why decisions were made
- Design a new feature considering all stakeholders

**Low Soft Skills:**
- Volunteer to mentor a junior
- Give a talk or write a doc
- Disagree with someone respectfully

**Low Ownership:**
- Take lead on a project
- Own the outcome (not just code)
- Take on problems others avoid

## Conversation with Your Manager

```
YOU: "I want to become a senior engineer. What do you think I need to work on?"

GOOD ANSWER: "You're solid technically. Focus on:
1. System design (you think in features, not systems)
2. Unblocking others (you solve your own problems well)
3. Taking bigger ownership (pick something to lead)"

BAD ANSWER: "Just keep doing good work"
(Vague. Ask for specifics.)

RED FLAG: "You're not ready"
(Without concrete next steps. Ask what's needed.)
```

## Growth Plan (6-12 months)

1. **Pick one area to master** (distributed systems, microservices, etc.)
2. **Lead a complex project** (2+ months, cross-team)
3. **Mentor a junior** (regular meetings, guide their growth)
4. **Give a talk** (internal or external)
5. **Own a critical system** (you're on-call, responsible)
6. **Make a big trade-off decision** (speed vs. quality, scale vs. simplicity)
7. **Improve a team process** (code review, deployment, monitoring)

After 6-12 months of this, ask for promotion.
